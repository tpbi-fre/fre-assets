# FRE Vending Machine Pilot Program
## Strategic Framework — LA Beta Test
### DRAFT v1 | April 4, 2026

---

## 1. THE OPPORTUNITY

FRE has a distribution bottleneck — 0.4% chain share despite strong velocity where stocked. Traditional retail requires buyer meetings, planogram negotiations, and 6-12 month onboarding cycles. Vending bypasses all of that.

**The thesis:** Brand-owned hardware creates a direct-to-consumer channel in high-traffic nightlife venues where nicotine pouches are a natural impulse purchase — and where traditional retail doesn't exist.

**Why bars specifically:**
- Captive audience (2-4 hour dwell time)
- Social/peer influence drives trial ("what's that?")
- 21+ environment solves age verification
- No competing shelf space — FRE owns 100% of the real estate
- Nightlife occasions align with pouch usage (discreet, no smoke/smell)

---

## 2. THE FORK: TWO PATHWAYS

### PATHWAY 1 — Owned Infrastructure
TPB owns the machine, controls the brand experience, captures the margin.

| | Track A: Pure FRE | Track B: FRE + ALP |
|---|---|---|
| **Product mix** | FRE only (all flavors/strengths) | FRE + ALP pouches |
| **Pros** | Maximum brand control, clean story, simpler logistics | Higher foot traffic to machine, broader consumer appeal, tests cross-portfolio pull |
| **Cons** | Lower variety may limit transactions | Dilutes FRE branding on machine; ALP positioning TBD |
| **Revenue model** | FRE margin only | FRE + ALP margin stacked |
| **Best for** | Brand-building + testing FRE pull in isolation | Maximizing machine profitability + testing multi-brand hypothesis |

**Pilot structure (both tracks):**
1. Place 1 machine in a high-traffic LA bar (PR agency connection)
2. Zero cost to bar owner — we supply machine + product
3. Alex serves as "Fed Rep" — weekly/biweekly check-ins, data pulls, restocking
4. 90-day test window to gather velocity, refill cadence, and owner feedback
5. Post-pilot: evaluate lease-to-own model where bar owner eventually captures up to 80% margin

### PATHWAY 2 — Third-Party Integration
Place FRE products within existing vending networks (e.g., NicBox, VapeTM, ZynVend).

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **What it looks like** | FRE occupies 4-6 slots in an existing nicotine vending machine alongside competitors |
| **Pros** | Zero capital, zero logistics, instant scale across existing network |
| **Cons** | No brand control, commodity positioning ("another player on the shelf"), shared with ZYN/Velo/On!, limited data access |
| **Revenue model** | Wholesale margin only — operator takes the rest |
| **Best for** | Quick distribution if owned model proves too capital-intensive |

**Recommendation:** Start with Pathway 1. Pathway 2 is the fallback if owned economics don't work, but it's strategically inferior — it's the equivalent of fighting for shelf space in a c-store, which is the exact problem we're trying to solve.

## 4. WHAT WE NEED TO LEARN

The pilot exists to answer these questions:

| Question | How We'll Answer It | Success Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| **What's the weekly velocity?** | Machine sales data | >$500/week to be viable |
| **What's the refill cadence?** | Restocking logs | Monthly or less = scalable; weekly = challenging |
| **Which products sell?** | SKU-level data | Validates flavor/strength assortment |
| **Will bar owners engage?** | Owner interviews | Willingness to participate in lease-to-own |
| **What's the real operational cost?** | Alex's time + travel + product logs | Must be <30% of revenue at scale |
| **Does FRE alone drive enough traffic?** | Track A vs Track B comparison (if running both) | Pure FRE must hit >70% of multi-brand velocity |

## 6. THE LEASE-TO-OWN EVOLUTION

Not for the pilot — but the long-term vision once we have velocity data:

**Phase 1 (Pilot):** TPB owns everything. Bar owner says yes, provides floor space, gets a small rev share (10-20%).

**Phase 2 (Partnership):** Bar owner buys inventory at wholesale, fills the machine themselves, keeps 60-80% of margin. TPB provides machine + brand + tech platform + subscription restocking.

**Phase 3 (Scale):** Lease-to-own model. Bar owner pays monthly lease that amortizes machine cost. After 24-36 months, they own the hardware outright. TPB becomes a product supplier + tech platform.

**The car analogy (from the call):** Like leasing a car — monthly payments chip away at the value, and at the end of the term you own it. Bar owner's incentive: they go from 20% margin to 80% margin once they own the hardware.
