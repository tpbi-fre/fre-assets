Store Parity
CA has near-identical shelf presence — the distribution is there, the volume isn't. Flavor is the variable.

California is the clearest proof point in the portfolio. 92% of Texas's store footprint, 56% of the volume. The only structural difference: Prop 31 limits FRE to a single unflavored SKU at retail. Texas runs the full 6-flavor lineup. That's not a distribution problem — it's a regulatory volume cap. And yet CA is still #2, still growing at 42% YoY. The demand is there. The law is the ceiling.
