THE THREE-CHANNEL THESIS
Events don't create instant sales — they ignite a chain reaction across channels
Step 1 · Awareness

Events Create DTC "Breadcrumbs"

Sampling events introduce FRE to a new audience. Some immediately order on Haypp — we see this as DTC spikes in event-week data, especially in markets with zero retail.

📊 3 of 28 events show >20% DTC lift within 4 weeks
Step 2 · Distribution

Demand Signal Unlocks Retail Doors

DTC demand in a ZIP code becomes a data point for retail buyers. 13,702 ZIPs have DTC orders but zero retail — that's a sell sheet for every chain buyer meeting.

📊 608 college ZIPs have DTC demand but no FRE retail (1.84M units)
Step 3 · Sales

Retail Absorbs the Long-Tail Volume

In markets with retail distribution, event-driven awareness converts at the gas station — not online. TX post-event: DTC flat, but retail grew +31% QoQ and Oracle shipments doubled.

📊 TX grew from 11.6% → 16.1% of national FRE volume in 6 months

The Retail Funnel Is Working in Texas

DTC showed -19% post-event — but that's 7 orders/week in a metro doing 636K cumulative DTC. The volume went to retail: NTC quarterly data shows TX outperformed national by 32 points in Q1 2026, and Oracle shipments to TX doubled from Oct to Feb. When someone samples FRE at UT Austin, they buy it at the Shell station the next morning.

WHERE EVENTS CONVERT: RETAIL PRESENCE × DTC LIFT
The pattern: events in distributed markets drive retail. Events in void markets drive DTC — or nothing.
💡

The Strategic Implication

Coordinate distribution expansion with event timing. Events in retail voids (Arizona, FSU, Missouri) generate awareness that has nowhere to convert. The best ROI comes from either: (1) events in markets with existing FRE retail, or (2) timing new store authorizations to coincide with event dates. The 7 "dark market" events represent ~$400K in DTC demand waiting for a retail door to walk into.
