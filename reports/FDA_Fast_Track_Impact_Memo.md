## TLDR

The FDA's fast-track nicotine pouch review pilot has stalled. TPB stock dropped 17% yesterday. Altria's on! PLUS is the **only** brand authorized under the pilot so far. FRE has two brands with applications in the pipeline. This memo explains what happened, what it means for FRE, and what to do about it.

---

## What Happened

On April 1, 2026, Reuters published an exclusive report revealing that the FDA's fast-track nicotine pouch authorization pilot — first reported by Reuters in 2025 — has stalled due to concerns from agency scientists.

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Pilot status** | Stalled. Decisions were expected by end of 2025 — now indefinitely delayed. |
| **Only authorization so far** | Altria's on! PLUS — 6 SKUs authorized December 2025 |
| **Still pending** | PMI (Zyn newer SKUs), BAT (Velo), TPB (FRE — 2 brands) |
| **Reason for delay** | FDA scientists concerned about: (1) youth uptake, (2) driving nicotine addiction in adults who don't already smoke |
| **FDA's position** | Applications "on track for faster decisions than most others" but declined to address youth concerns |
| **Market impact** | PMI −4.8%, BAT −1.5%, **TPB −17%** (April 1) |

---

## Why TPB Got Hit Hardest

TPB's 17% drop vs. PMI's 4.8% is disproportionate but explainable:

1. **FRE is a bigger % of TPB's valuation story** — pouches are TPB's growth engine. For PMI, Zyn pouches are huge but diversified across a massive portfolio. For TPB, FRE *is* the growth narrative.
2. **Two brands in the pilot** — market read is that FRE's PMTA path just got longer and less certain.
3. **Small-cap volatility** — TPB's lower market cap amplifies moves. A few institutional sellers can move the stock 10%+.
4. **on! PLUS just got authorized AND went national** — perception that Altria got the green light while everyone else is stuck. That's a competitive moat, not just a regulatory win.
