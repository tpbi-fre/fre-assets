## Why This Matters Now

UK nicotine pouch category is in hypergrowth and largely uncontested at the premium tier. The disposable vape ban (June 2025) is actively pushing consumers toward pouches. FRE has zero UK presence — this brief outlines what entry would require.

## Regulatory Landscape (as of March 2026)

| Issue | Status |
|---|---|
| Legal status | ✅ Legal — regulated under General Product Safety Regulations 2005 (GPSR) |
| Age of sale requirement | ❌ No national legal minimum — voluntary retailer standard (18+) |
| Nicotine strength caps | ⚠️ Under review — government considering capping at 20mg (current UK vape limit); ZYN's 50mg may be restricted |
| Tobacco/vape regulations apply? | ❌ No — TRPR 2016 and MHRA oversight does NOT currently cover pouches |
| Ban risk | Low — no ban announced, but regulatory tightening expected 2026–2027 |

**Key Risk:** FRE's full strength stack (3–15mg) is safe under current rules. If 20mg cap passes, FRE stays unaffected — this could actually hurt ZYN's high-strength dominance.

## Entry Considerations

### Channel Strategy (Recommended Sequence)
1. **DTC first (frepouch.com/uk)** — zero retail complexity, age-gate built in, read the consumer
2. **Specialty online retail** (Alternix, VapeUK-type nicotine retailers) — fastest volume
3. **Convenience / forecourts** — follow ZYN's distribution path once brand is known

### Regulatory Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Register products under GPSR with UK responsible party (post-Brexit = separate from EU)
- [ ] Ensure packaging meets UK English labeling standards
- [ ] Add nicotine content labeling (voluntary best practice, expected to become mandatory)
- [ ] Age verification on DTC site (UK standard: 18+)
- [ ] Monitor Tobacco and Vapes Bill progress — may extend age-of-sale laws to pouches

### Pricing Signal
UK pouch prices: £5–£8 per tin standard. Premium tins (VELO x McLaren LE) reaching £12–£15. FRE should enter at £6.99–£7.99 to signal premium without being inaccessible.
