## 2. THE CASE FOR "SMOOTH" (THE NAME)

### Why "Sweet" doesn't work:
- **Generic** — doesn't communicate anything specific. What does "sweet" taste like?
- **Category confusion** — consumers can't distinguish it from other "sweet" products
- **Low shelf appeal** — no flavor personality to attract trial
- **No brand voice** — "Sweet" doesn't align with FRE's premium, distinctive positioning

### Why "Smooth" works:
- **Sensory** — communicates a texture/experience, not just a taste
- **Differentiated** — no major competitor uses "Smooth" as a flavor name
- **Premium signal** — smooth implies crafted, refined, intentional
- **Brand-aligned** — fits FRE's "Own Your Edge" voice (smooth IS having your edge under control)
- **Trial-friendly** — invites curiosity: "what does smooth taste like?" vs. "oh, sweet, boring"

### Competitive naming landscape:
| Brand | Their version | Name |
|---|---|---|
| ZYN | Unflavored/Sweet | "Smooth" (they use it!) |
| on! | Sweet/Unflavored | "Original" |
| Velo | Sweet | "Smooth Honey" / sweet variants |
| Rogue | Sweet | "Wintergreen Smooth" / "Sweet" |
| Lucy | Sweet | "Sweet Cinnamon" etc. |
| **FRE** | Sweet → **Smooth** | **Clean, simple, distinctive** |

## 5. STRATEGIC RATIONALE

### Why Smooth matters for the portfolio:
1. **Consumer choice completeness** — Mint, Wintergreen, Smooth gives FRE a clean "core three" that covers the top flavor preferences: cooling, herbal/bold, and mild/sweet
2. **Mint cannibalization hedge** — as Peppermint LTO launches July 4, Smooth provides an entirely different profile for consumers who don't want intense cooling
3. **Retail shelf story** — "We've got a core three now" is a better TBM pitch than "we added another mint variant"
4. **$0 execution cost** — it's literally a relabel. The risk/reward ratio is absurd.
5. **Speed** — packaging is done. No formulation. No PMTA. This can move as fast as the supply chain allows.

### Consumer persona:
The Smooth consumer isn't the same as the Mint/Wintergreen consumer. They want:
- A mild, non-intense experience
- Something they can use all day without flavor fatigue
- Often a first-time pouch user who's intimidated by strong mint/wintergreen
- Often a female consumer (sweet/smooth indexes higher with women across the category)

### Competitive gap:
- ZYN's "Smooth" is well-established — proving the segment exists
- on!'s "Original" is a top seller — proving unflavored/mild sells at scale
- FRE's "Sweet" is in-market but invisible — a rename + marketing push can unlock existing demand
