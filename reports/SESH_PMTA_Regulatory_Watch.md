## What "Substantive Scientific Review" Means

This is NOT an authorization. The FDA has determined:
1. ✅ Application is complete (all required data submitted)
2. ⏳ Scientific review begins (toxicology, abuse liability, population-level health impact)
3. ❓ Marketing order decision (authorize, deny, or request more data)

**Precedent:** ZYN's PMTA review took approximately 2 years from acceptance to authorization. Sesh's 64-SKU bundle is more complex.

## Why This Matters for FRE

### 1. Synthetic Nicotine Precedent
Sesh uses synthetic (tobacco-free) nicotine. If authorized, it establishes that synthetic nicotine products can meet the FDA's public health standard. This could:
- Open the door for other synthetic nicotine brands
- Create a "tobacco-free" marketing claim that resonates with health-conscious consumers
- Potentially complicate the regulatory landscape for tobacco-derived nicotine products like FRE

### 2. Category Legitimacy
More authorized products = more mainstream acceptance of nicotine pouches. Long-term positive for the category, including FRE.

### 3. 64 SKUs = Flavor Breadth Threat
If authorized, Sesh would instantly have the **largest FDA-authorized flavor portfolio** in the category. For context:
- ZYN: 20 authorized SKUs
- on! PLUS: 6 authorized SKUs
- Sesh (if authorized): 64 SKUs

### 4. Timeline Consideration
12–18 month review means Sesh authorization wouldn't hit until mid-to-late 2027. This gives FRE time to:
- Advance its own PMTA strategy
- Establish FRE Labs products in market
- Build brand loyalty before the competitive set expands
